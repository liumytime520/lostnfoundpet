If you want to reunite the pet by yourself, here are websites for posting found pets: (these are not all but where most pet losted persons look/post on)

Facebook groups

Auckland Missing&Found Pets

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

A place where people in a auckland area who cares about missing pets or experienced missing pets stays in to help look for other's missing pets. In this group, users can post for lost and found pet.

South Auckland Pets Lost & Found

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

South Auckland Pets Lost & Found is a community-managed Facebook group serving South Auckland (Manukau area). It allows individuals to post about lost or found pets—especially cats and dogs, though other pets like rabbits can also be included. It’s not run by any formal organization, but by local people supporting each other with timely alerts.

Lost And Found Pets Auckland Central

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

Lost And Found Pets – Auckland Central is a community-run Facebook (or social media) group dedicated to helping owners reunite with lost and found pets in the Auckland Central area. This platform allows individuals—not formal organizations—to post alerts about missing or found animals such as cats, dogs, rabbits, and potentially other companion pets. Members share photos, descriptions, locations, and contact details to facilitate quick reunions, with group members and neighbours helping by monitoring posts and sharing with their networks.

Lost and Found Pets - New Zealand

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

Lost and Found Pets – New Zealand is a community network and set of online services that help reunite missing pets with their owners. People can report lost or found cats, dogs, rabbits, and other pets. Listings are posted mainly by individuals, and shared across platforms like NZ Companion Animal Register (LostPet.co.nz), Lost Pet Finders, Trade Me, and Facebook groups.

Website specialise listing lost and found pets

Lost Pet Listing

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

A Lost Pet Listing is a public notice (online or offline) created by an owner or finder, showing a missing pet’s details and contact information to help the community reunite the animal with its family.

SPCA Found Animal Form

Click the picture to get to the website

The SPCA Found Animal protocol in New Zealand involves either contacting your nearest SPCA centre directly if the animal is sick or vulnerable, or—if the animal appears healthy—taking steps such as checking for a microchip, using paper collars, posting to animalregister.co.nz/lostpet, distributing flyers, and utilizing platforms like Trade Me or local community pages; there is no downloadable “found animal” form, but clear guidance is provided on SPCA’s Advice & Welfare pages.

Rare but not impossible places

Trade Me – Community > Pets > Lost & Found

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

Trade Me – Lost & Found Pets is a section under Community > Pets > Lost & Found where individuals can post notices about missing or found animals. It covers all types of pets, including cats, dogs, rabbits, and birds. Owners or finders upload photos, details, and contact information so the wider New Zealand community can help reunite pets with their families.

Neighbourly

Click the picture to get to the website

Neighbourly is a New Zealand community website and app where locals connect with their neighbours. People use it to share news, ask for help, recommend services, and post about lost or found pets such as cats, dogs, or rabbits. Listings are made by individuals, not organisations, so neighbours can directly support each other

Paw Boost

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

PawBoost is an online lost-and-found pet service widely used in the US and internationally. Individuals can create free “Lost Pet” or “Found Pet” alerts with photos and details. These alerts are shared through PawBoost’s website, app, email, and large Facebook community network to increase visibility. It covers all types of pets (mostly cats and dogs, but also rabbits and other animals). PawBoost is not an organisation that rescues pets itself—it connects pet owners, finders, and local communities to help reunite them.

Reddit

Click the picture to get to the website

How to sign in

Reddit is a global online discussion platform where users post content in topic-specific communities called “subreddits.” In New Zealand, communities like r/auckland or r/newzealand are sometimes used to post about lost or found pets, including cats, dogs, rabbits, and other animals. Posts are made by individuals, not organisations, allowing the local community to share sightings, advice, and help reunite pets with their owners.

Key notes:

Do not assume its friendly, don’t let your own pet or children approach it.

Do not assume it’s abandoned — it could be a beloved pet that’s lost.

Never sell or rehome a found pet yourself — always try to find the owner first.
